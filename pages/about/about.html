Code

All main site functionality runs on pure HTML and CSS with no javascript (...mostly. I still use some minor javascript for some inconsequential unnecessary stuff that would be impossible otherwise, and external embeds that require it (I'm looking at you YouTube)). That might mean I have to do some strange hacks in the "code" at times but if it works it works.

My main reason for this is because I hate it when websites use javascript for absolutely everything. That slows down loading speeds significantly and wastes computing power for no reason, and most of the time they are probably doing other garbage with it becaues of course they would. I turn off javascript by default and it's always so dumb when a page is just completely blank unless I enable it. Back in the day people optimized their websites to no end because you only had some kilobytes of download speed max, but now that we are in the future I guess everyone decided that they missed the loading speeds of the past so they wanted to bring it back. It's a similar scenario to optimization in games I'd say, especially with prebuilt engines.

Anyways what that means is that you don't need javascript to see any of this. Feel free to turn it off even, it pretty much won't make a difference. In fact you should just go ahead and turn it off everywhere by default (like me) to stop dumb websites from doing dumb things that they don't need to be doing. Oh and while you're at it you should also get UBlock Origin to block the rest of the garbage that all these loaded websites like piling on (stuff like dumb scripts and trackers and ads). You might need a firefox-based browser though, I think Chromium browsers finally killed it because it does something useful and Google can't have that.

Theme

The site theme was made entirely by me from scratch. It's not the most amazing or intricate or modern thing ever but it's mostly cohesive at least. The No-Javascript rule also prevents me from doing anything too fancy anyways though.

I originally designed the base concept of the theme for something completely unrelated that I won't get into yet. At the time it was simpler and less efficient but that's because it didn't really need to be much. Now that I am reusing it for this I put more care into it, though some residual stuff from that time is still present in the code as of writing this.

If you look at the background here you will see a noise pattern (well not "pattern" by definition but you get it). Originally I created that I think purely to fill the space better than a solid color could. It adds detail for free basically, but I also liked how it looked so I kept going with it. The noise itself originally started as raw images for each color, then it transitioned to a single image that was tinted, and then finally into an SVG that generates the noise automatically. The SVG may or may not be more efficient than the second method (both are decently fast regardless), but the SVG method is more reusable and modifyable so I'm sticking with it for now. It also prevents the noise pattern from repeating since it's not just a single image (though that was not too noticable anyway).


Logo

The logo is also a remnant from that original use case I mentioned earlier. I created it because I felt like I needed something to spice the page up, so I came up with this geometric cuboid shape thing design. At first I made it simply in a raster image editor, but later I turned it into a full 3D model because it would be easier to deal with. As opposed to raster or even vector images, a 3D model allows me to change the actual shape better since it is actually 3D instead of faking it, and it also allows me to easily change the shading and lighting if I need to.

You may have also noticed that the cuboid icon thing subtly changes depending on what page you are on. These icon variants actually have a specific meaning, and you may have picked up on it if you really pay attention. If you haven't however, here is a listing of all of them and what they coorespond to:

Most of these should be self-explanatory, apart from "Special Case". Originally it was essentially unused and I decided it would be for anything else that doesn't fit the other 3 categories, but as I was writing this I remembered that I had some beta pages on this site that aren't ready yet but I still published (for those of you curious enough to find them). That sounded like a perfect fit for "Special Case", and so that's it's new usage.

As for their numbering, it's based on the number of edges created when the shape was cut out of the cube. For -1 the resulting cutout is round so technically it could be said that the edge count is infinite (or 0 but that is taken), but I went with a simpler number for text formatting reasons.

Apart from these I also made another version with a 9-edge cutout, but I didn't like how it looked since the others had a nicer symmetry with the cube's geometry. Specifically, a hexagon and triangle can be made nicely from an intersecting plane (The actual shapes are just derivative from this approach), and the "curved" one is essentially just a variant of the triangle. Alternatively you could say that the 9-side variant is one step closer to approaching the curved version (for n-edges as n approaches infinity). So rather than include all possible cutouts past the first 2 nice ones (3 and 6), we may as well just use the infinite one instead. Here it is anyway though:

There's also the scenario of changing how many vertices are "anchored" when you approach infinity, but in our case the geometry of the cube limits that to a hard 3 no matter what. With some careful modeling I suppose you could create an interesting curve with more anchored points, but I don't think that would look right, the same as the 9-edge variant. In fact the 9-edge variant already appears to have a different curve-profile to it's supposed limit of the -1 variant. This is why I only finalized the main 4 above, they were simply the nicest ones you could make given the geometry of a cube. Perhaps there is some alternative geometry other than these cutouts I could attempt, but for the moment I don't need any more so the main 4 stay.
