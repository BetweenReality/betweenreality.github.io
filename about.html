About the site

This website is the hub for all my publicly known projects and other activities

All main site functionality runs on pure HTML and CSS with no javascript, meaning the experience should be essentially identical if you have it off in your browser (I only use javascript when absolutely necessary, otherwise I avoid it if I can.) That might mean I have to do some strange hacks in the "code" but if it works it works.

You may have also noticed that the main cuboid thing icon subtly changes depending on what page you are on. Special Case

Most of these should be self-explanatory, apart from "Special Case". Originally it was essentially unused and I decided it would be for anything else that doesn't fit the other 3 categories, but as I was writing this I remembered that I had some beta pages on this site that aren't ready yet but I still published (for those of you curious enough to find them). That sounded like a perfect fit for "Special Case", and so that is it's new usage.

As for their numbering, it is based on the number of edges created when the shape was cut out of the cube. For -1 the resulting cutout is round so it could be said that the edge count is infinite, but I went with a simpler number for text formatting reasons.

Apart from these I also made another version with a 9-edge cutout, but I didn't like how it looked since the others had a nicer symmetry with the cube's geometry. Specifically, The hexagon and triangle can be made nicely from an intersecting plane, and the "curved" one is essentially just a variant of the triangle, or alternatively, you could say that the 9-side variant is one step closer to approaching the curved version as well (for n-edges as n approaches infinity). So rather than include all possible cutouts past the first 2 nice ones (3 and 6), we may as well just use the infinite one instead. Here it is anyway though:

Logo 9

Unused

There's also the scenario of changing how many vertices are "anchored" when you approach infinity, but in our case the geometry of the cube limits that to a hard 3 no matter what. With some careful modeling I suppose you could create an interesting curve with more anchored points, but I don't think that would look right, the same as the 9-edge variant. This is why I only finalized the main 4 above, they were simply the nicest ones you could make given the geometry of a cube.
